Performance Health & Activity Synchronization Engine (P.H.A.S.E)

The P.H.A.S.E system is a post-game dashboard I developed for Kerala Blasters F.C in the Indian Super League. The dashboard looks to elevate player development and optimize performance by providing a comprehensive view of player metrics, combining training and match performance. We looked to track key indicators such as workload, heart rate, and physical recovery to ensure athletes are performing at their peak. Safety & Quality Indicator Set (SQuIS)

SQuIS is a dashboard tracking 40 Safety & Quality indicators across various healthcare groups, utilizing the Making Data Count approach. It features funnel plots and Statistical Process Control (SPC) charts to benchmark hospitals statewide, while SPC charts monitor individual hospital performance over a 3-year period, identifying special cause variations. Data is pulled through a Power BI dataflow ETL process, with R used to generate MISC charts for each indicator at the hospital level. Additionally, I created a monthly hospital report for Health Service Providers, which is used for board reporting and visible to all public healthcare workers in the state.

Diabetes Patient Classification

I collaboratively worked on a project to classify diabetic patients, covering five phases: Data Extraction, Transformation, Machine Learning, Integration, and Validation. In the Transformation phase, I used spaCy and MedSpaCy to build a Natural Language Processing (NLP) model in SnowPark to identify diabetic patients from discharge summaries. Initially using Azure Databricks, I switched to SnowPark for better computational efficiency. I also led the Integration team, creating a Power BI dashboard with Power BI dataflows to display ML model performance, explain classifications, and highlight undiagnosed diabetic patients, aiding clinicians in preparing for rising admissions.

FIFA World Cup 2022 Simulator

For my FIFA World Cup 2022 prediction project, I built a Bayesian model using historical match data as the prior, simulating each match 500 times and running 100 overall tournament simulations to predict the winner and common progressions. The results indicated Brazil as the winner, with France as the runners-up. The model included 50% random weight distribution, experimented with varying head-to-head weights (settling on 30%), and incorporated player ranking weights to account for individual performance, refining match outcomes.
